Soccer

Posted Mar 1

I've touched upon soccer being one of my interests, so I want to expand on that part of my life because I feel it has taught me many valuable lessons. I was once on a team when I was younger, playing for the East Meadow Soccer Club. I played in that club for a few years before I decided to quit. During my time, I was always in the starting 11, playing at the left wing. My coach called me a speed demon for my pace, and that made me feel like I was important.

I remember feeling so anxious before every game, wanting to not just do my best but to also be the best, to stand out. So much so, I was so nervous to make any mistake, anything that could lead to a turnover in favor of the opposing team. That being said, I barely dribbled, barely went to score, and barely tried to do anything, leaving my team one man short.

Throughout the years I spent on that team, starting in every single match, I only scored one goal. Not because I didn't try, but because I didn't try to mess up to learn.

Looking back, I wish I would've taken those risks because I love the sport. It taught me that you need to fail countless times before you get it right. And then there will still be failures to learn from. It taught me risks need to be taken to see how far you can go. To be humble, have discipline, but not to be shy. Because at the end of the day it really is just a game.
